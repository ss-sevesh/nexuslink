## Hypothesis Statement

If word embeddings (WMT) capture latent semantic relationships between linguistic units, and these relationships can be treated as complex networks (BLEU), then multi-task neural models trained on linguistic structure can outperform those relying solely on surface-level features by at least 10% in tasks such as language modeling and machine translation.

## Suggested Experiments

1. Fine-tune pre-trained transformer architecture (e.g. BERT) to jointly optimize for linguistic structure and surface-level features on a large-scale dataset.
2. Compare the performance of multi-task neural models trained on different combinations of linguistic tasks; measure improvements in accuracy by at least 10% over baseline models relying solely on surface-level features.
3. Investigate the impact of varying the complexity of linguistic structure representations (e.g. graph-based vs. tensor-based) on model performance and computational efficiency.
