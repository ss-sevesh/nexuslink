## Hypothesis Statement

If deep learning's ability to learn hierarchical representations ( cs.LG ) and multi-head attention's ability to selectively focus on relevant input features (cs.CV) are both manifestations of complex, nonlinear interactions between multiple variables, then a neural network with a depth of at least 12 layers and a multi-head attention mechanism with at least 64 heads will exhibit an exponential increase in performance on tasks requiring sequential processing of variable-length inputs.

## Suggested Experiments

1. Train a 12-layer ResNet-152 with multi-head attention on the task of sentence classification; measure accuracy and computational efficiency
2. Compare performance of single-head vs. multi-head attention in a transformer network on tasks requiring sequential processing, such as machine translation or text summarization
3. Investigate whether incorporating hierarchical representations from deep learning into the attention mechanism can improve performance on tasks with variable-length inputs
