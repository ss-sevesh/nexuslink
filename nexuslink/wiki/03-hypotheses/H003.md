## Hypothesis Statement

If the language model's ability to generalize from few-shot examples (SimCSE-BERTbase) is a manifestation of its capacity for hierarchical abstraction, then similarly-designed neural architectures should be able to learn effective parameterizations for nonlinear dynamical systems.

## Suggested Experiments

1. Implement a variant of SimCSE-BERTbase on the Lotka-Volterra equations; measure its ability to predict population dynamics over long timescales.
2. Compare performance of the hierarchical abstraction model with traditional symbolic regression methods for a set of benchmark dynamical systems.
3. Investigate the extent to which introducing physically-motivated inductive biases (e.g. symplectic structure) improves the model's predictive accuracy.
